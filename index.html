Mahabharat

Author: Vyasa

Genre: Epic, Mythology

Published: Ancient India (circa 400 BCE - 400 CE)

Description: One of the greatest epics of Indian history, the *Mahabharat* narrates the story of the Kuru dynasty, the rivalry between the Pandavas and Kauravas, and the great war of Kurukshetra. It contains the Bhagavad Gita and offers profound philosophical and ethical lessons.

Pride and Prejudice

Author: Jane Austen

Genre: Romance, Classic Literature

Published: 1813

Description: A timeless novel exploring themes of love, social class, and marriage in 19th-century England. The witty and independent Elizabeth Bennet navigates her complex relationship with the proud but honorable Mr. Darcy.

Harry Potter and the Sorcerer’s Stone

Author: J.K. Rowling

Genre: Fantasy, Adventure

Published: 1997

Description: The first book in the Harry Potter series follows the young orphan Harry as he discovers he is a wizard and begins his journey at Hogwarts. With his newfound friends Ron and Hermione, he unravels the mystery of the Sorcerer’s Stone.

Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets

Author: J.K. Rowling

Genre: Fantasy, Adventure

Published: 1998

Description: Harry’s second year at Hogwarts is threatened by a terrifying monster lurking within the Chamber of Secrets. As students are petrified one by one, Harry must uncover the truth before it's too late.

The Philosophy of Money

Author: Georg Simmel

Genre: Philosophy, Sociology

Published: 1900

Description: This book explores the relationship between money and social interactions, providing deep insights into how economic structures shape human behavior, social institutions, and cultural values.

Rich Dad Poor Dad

Author: Robert T. Kiyosaki

Genre: Personal Finance, Self-Help

Published: 1997

Description: This bestseller contrasts the financial philosophies of two father figures: Kiyosaki's own educated but financially struggling father (*Poor Dad*) and his friend’s wealthy, financially savvy father (*Rich Dad*). The book provides valuable insights on financial literacy, investing, and building wealth through smart money management.

Think and Grow Rich

Author: Napoleon Hill

Genre: Self-Help, Personal Development

Published: 1937

Description: Based on decades of research, this book lays out 13 principles for achieving success, wealth, and personal development. Napoleon Hill studied successful individuals, including Andrew Carnegie and Henry Ford, to create a timeless guide to financial and personal growth.

The Richest Man in Babylon

Author: George S. Clason

Genre: Personal Finance, Self-Help

Published: 1926

Description: This book presents financial wisdom through engaging parables set in ancient Babylon. Covering wealth-building strategies, saving principles, and investment tips, it remains one of the most influential personal finance books of all time.
